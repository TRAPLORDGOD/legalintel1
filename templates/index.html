LegalIntel Whitepaper

LegalIntel uses Artificial Intelligence to enhance the productivity of law firms and lawyers. Like ChatGPT, LEGALINTEL is built on a version of OpenAI's GPT AI. Unlike ChatGPT, LEGALINTEL supports legal work. How? Starting with general internet data from the GPT model, LEGALINTEL was further trained with general legal data (including case law and reference materials). When engaged by a firm, LEGALINTEL is then trained by the firm's own work products and templates (much like a new employee's onboarding when joining a law firm!).

LEGALINTEL assists with contract analysis, due diligence, litigation, and regulatory compliance and can help generate insights, recommendations, and predictions based on data. As a result, lawyers can deliver faster and more cost-effective solutions for client issues. We have trained our model on the supreme court and high courts data and cases. We also provide assistance with contracts.

Key Features of LegalIntel:

  • Advanced contract analysis
  • Detailed due diligence support
  • Litigation case analysis and prediction
  • Regulatory compliance assistance
  • Insights, recommendations, and predictions based on data

How LegalIntel Works:

LegalIntel leverages cutting-edge AI technology to revolutionize the legal industry. Here's a brief overview of how it works:

  1. Training: LegalIntel is initially trained on general internet data using OpenAI's GPT model. It is then further trained with specific legal data, including case law and reference materials.
  2. Firm-specific Training: When engaged by a law firm, LegalIntel undergoes training using the firm's own work products and templates. This ensures that the AI understands the firm's specific practices and requirements.
  3. Application: Once trained, LegalIntel can assist with various legal tasks, including contract analysis, due diligence, litigation support, and regulatory compliance.
  4. Insights and Recommendations: LegalIntel generates valuable insights, recommendations, and predictions based on the analyzed data. This empowers lawyers to make informed decisions and deliver high-quality solutions to their clients.
